About Me

I am a fourth year PhD candidate in MIT's Operations Research Center. I am co-advised by Retsef Levi and Georgia Perakis , and supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. During my PhD, I spent a summer as an intern with IBM Food Trust under IBM Blockchain.

Before coming to MIT, I received a B.S. in Math, B.S. in Statistics, and M.A. in Math from the Pennsylvania State University, where I was advised by Christopher Griffin. After graduation, I spent a year working at Booz Allen Hamilton as a data scientist. I have also worked briefly at Bates White and in the Risk and Decision Science Team of the Environmental Laboratory, U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center.

Research Interests

Generally, I am interested in supply chain coordination problems and the design of interventions to achieve socially optimal outcomes. I am most interested in applications in the public and non-profit sectors. Much of my current research focuses on supply chain interventions for creating better access to, and consumption of, fresh food.
