I am a fifth year PhD candidate at the Operations Research Center at MIT. I am co-advised by Retsef Levi and Georgia Perakis. I am interested in data-driven policy making and the design of interventions for social good. The central aim of my work is the development of new analytical methods that have impact in the public and non-profit sectors. My current research focuses on supply chain and policy interventions for creating better access to, and consumption of, fresh food. Much of this work has been motivated through collaborations with the Fair Food Network, the Boston Area Gleaners, and the Massachusetts Department of Transitional Assistance. During my PhD, I also spent a summer as an intern with IBM Food Trust under IBM Blockchain.

Before coming to MIT, I received a B.S. in Math, B.S. in Statistics, and M.A. in Math from the Pennsylvania State University, where I was advised by Christopher Griffin. After graduation, I spent a year working at Booz Allen Hamilton as a data scientist. I have also worked briefly at Bates White Economic Consulting and in the Risk and Decision Science Team of the Environmental Laboratory, U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center.
